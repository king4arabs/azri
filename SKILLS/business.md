# SKILLS — Business

## Purpose
Sustain AZRI: revenue, partnerships, fundraising, and strategic positioning aligned with healthcare reality.

## What good looks like
- Multi-tier revenue from B2C (patient/caregiver), B2B (doctor/clinic), and B2B2C (institution/insurer).
- Clear unit economics per tier and region.
- Partnerships chosen for distribution, trust, and integration — not vanity logos.
- Fundraising decks honest about the regulated, long-cycle nature of healthcare.

## Standards
- Pricing reflects real value and KSA willingness to pay.
- Contracts include DPAs, BAA-equivalents, audit rights for institutional customers.
- Investor updates monthly; quarterly board materials with cohort + ARR + retention.
- Strategic decisions land in `DECISIONS.md` if they materially shape the architecture or product.

## Anti-patterns
- Selling "compliance" we don't have.
- Naming partners we don't formally have agreements with.
- Pricing that ignores Mada-only customers (KSA payment realities).
- Free tiers that consume support resources unsustainably.

## How to implement
- Sales motion playbooks per persona; CRM hygiene from day one.
- Pilot agreements with clear success criteria and exit ramps.
- Procurement-ready collateral kit: security overview, DPA template, technical FAQ.

## How to audit / test
- Quarterly business review against ROADMAP and PROJECT_STATUS.
- Pipeline & funnel review monthly.
- Cohort retention and NRR reviewed each board cycle.

## How to scale
- Channel partnerships in GCC.
- Reseller / system integrator model for hospital procurement complexity.
- Insurer outcome-linked pricing as evidence accumulates.

## AZRI-specific notes
- Healthcare sales cycles are long; cash-flow planning reflects that.
- Government/health-authority engagement is a separate motion with separate timelines.
- Patient pricing is sensitive: avoid signaling that monitoring is a luxury.

## Open questions
- B2C pricing in SAR vs USD presentation.
- Outcome-based contracting with insurers — model and evidence gates.
- Co-branding policy with hospital partners.
